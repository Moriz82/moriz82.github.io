Printing a Satellite Antenna for NOAA & GOES

January 18, 2025 6 minute read
SDR RF Python

This antenna started as a paper napkin sketch, a spool of PETG, and a cheap RTL-SDR v4 dongle. My goal was a travel-ready rig that could backpack into remote sites yet still capture weather imagery clean enough to plot storm movement in real time.

Designing the Helical

I modelled the boom, supports, and reflector in Fusion 360, then exported each part for slicing. The reflector sits 0.25Î» behind the first turn to keep axial ratio tight, while brass inserts hold the copper wire in place without deforming under heat.

First-Light Decoding

GNU Radio handles the demodulation chain and pipes the audio into wxtoimg. The block diagram below is simple but robust enough to survive noisy captures.

from gnuradio import blocks, filter, analog, gr
from satnogs.helpers import doppler_shift

samp_rate = 2_400_000
freq = 137_912_500

top_block = gr.top_block()
source = blocks.file_source(gr.sizeof_gr_complex, "capture_2025-01-18.cfile")
lo = analog.sig_source_c(samp_rate, analog.GR_COS_WAVE, doppler_shift(freq), 1.0)
mixer = blocks.multiply_cc()
channel = filter.freq_xlating_fir_filter_ccc(5, filter.firdes.low_pass(1, samp_rate, 34e3, 12e3), 0, samp_rate)

top_block.connect(source, (mixer, 0))
top_block.connect(lo, (mixer, 1))
top_block.connect(mixer, channel)
top_block.connect(channel, blocks.wavfile_sink("noaa-18.wav", 1, int(samp_rate / 5)))
top_block.run()

Save the intermediate complex samples. When NOAA or GOES pushes new telemetry formats, you can re-run the captures through updated demodulators without waiting for the next pass.

Cleaning the Imagery

The last step is a Python notebook that stitches the APT channel data into full-resolution PNGs. Histogram equalisation plus a simple coastline overlay makes the frames shareable.

Handheld rigs hate urban RF noise. If your downlinks look like grey mush, bring a 10dB LNA and move the entire setup at least one block away from power lines.

What's Next

Next revs include an auto-rotor, TDR-based tuning script, and packaging the demod pipeline into a Docker image so I can process imagery on a fanless mini PC in the field.
